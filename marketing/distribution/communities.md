# Community & launch-board kit — Indie Hackers, Peerlist, Uneed & co

These are *community* channels: they reward stories and numbers, not ads.
The currency is honesty — small real numbers beat big vague claims.

---

## 1. Indie Hackers — do this today (~25 min)

**Why:** high-quality founder/dev audience, and IH pages rank on Google
permanently. Milestone posts with real numbers are the native format.

**Setup:** indiehackers.com → sign up → add product:
- **Name:** Jot
- **Tagline:** Free, open-source dictation that never leaves your Mac
- **Website:** https://jot-transcribe.com/?utm_source=indiehackers&utm_medium=community&utm_campaign=profile

**Milestone post — ready to paste (post today, while the 100 milestone is real):**

> **Title:** My free Mac dictation app just crossed 100 downloads — here's the system I built to get to 100/day
>
> I launched Jot in mid-April: a free, open-source dictation app for macOS.
> Press a hotkey, speak, the text lands at your cursor — transcription runs
> fully on-device on the Apple Neural Engine. No account, no subscription.
> The paid competitors charge $8–15/month for this.
>
> Today it crossed 100 total downloads. Tiny number, real milestone.
>
> What I've learned so far as a solo dev doing everything:
>
> 1. **Posts decay, listings compound.** My social posts get a day of
>    traffic. The Homebrew tap and directory listings keep working.
> 2. **"Free + open source + on-device" does the selling.** I don't write
>    persuasion — I write facts and the privacy crowd shows up.
> 3. **I automated the marketing grind.** A scheduled AI agent ships me a
>    brief every morning at 5:30: download stats, threads worth answering,
>    ready-to-post content with visuals. I review and click Post. ~10
>    min/day of marketing instead of 2 hours.
>
> Goal: 100 downloads/day. Currently at ~2/day, so a 50x gap — the plan is
> directories + SEO comparison pages + one good Product Hunt launch.
>
> What moved the needle for your first real traction — launches or the
> slow compounding stuff?

(Ends with a question on purpose — IH ranks by comment activity.)

**Cadence after that:** one post per week max (next week: the AI-marketing-
agent system as its own story — IH loves process posts). Spend 5 min/day
commenting on others' posts; IH karma follows reciprocity.

## 2. Peerlist Launchpad — launch next Monday (~15 min)

peerlist.io/launchpad — weekly launch board for dev tools, much easier to
place top-5 than Product Hunt, free, dev-heavy audience (right crowd for a
Mac utility). Launches start Mondays. Reuse the Product Hunt kit copy
(tagline, description, gallery) from [product-hunt.md](product-hunt.md).
A good Peerlist result is also a dry run for the PH launch.

## 3. Uneed — submit now, it queues (~10 min)

uneed.best — Product Hunt alternative with a daily launch calendar; free
submissions queue a few weeks out. Submit today with the PH kit copy and
let the queue work. Use `?utm_source=uneed`.

## 4. Microlaunch — same drill (~10 min)

microlaunch.net — monthly leaderboard, small but real dev traffic.
Same assets, `?utm_source=microlaunch`.

## 5. Dev.to / Hashnode — the engineering story (when ready)

A technical write-up ranks on Google for years: "Running NVIDIA's Parakeet
on the Apple Neural Engine", "The clipboard sandwich: pasting text into any
Mac app", "Building a Dynamic Island for macOS". The daily agent can draft
these — ask it via the repo when you want one.

## Skip / later

- **BetaList** — pre-launch products only; Jot is launched. Skip.
- **Product Hunt** — kit ready in [product-hunt.md](product-hunt.md), still
  gated on demo video + a free day. Do Peerlist first as the rehearsal.
- **r/SideProject, r/indiehackers (Reddit)** — fine occasionally; the daily
  agent already covers Reddit pacing, don't double-post the same week.

## The one rule everywhere

Post → then live in the comments for the first hour. On every one of these
platforms, the algorithm and the audience reward the founder who answers.
